# SpikeForge Imagery

> Photography that reflects the confidence, warmth, and professionalism of modern coaching.

## Strategic Foundation

**Brand Alignment:** SpikeForge imagery embodies The Guide archetype by pairing mission clarity with operational confidence. The visual tone blends three influences: generous editorial whitespace and human purpose, premium product polish with bold compositional confidence, and enterprise-grade service credibility. Images show real coaching work and practical systems in action, never generic corporate stock.

## Photography Direction

### Overall Tone
- **Confident, not corporate:** Professionals who are relaxed in their expertise, not stiff or posed
- **Warm, not casual:** Human warmth with professional context — coaching offices, modern workspaces, quiet meeting rooms
- **Focused, not busy:** Clean compositions with a single clear subject or interaction
- **Real, not staged:** Authentic moments of coaching, conversation, and reflection
- **Modern, not trendy:** Contemporary aesthetics that age well

### Lighting
- Natural light preferred — window light, soft overhead
- Warm, even tones — no harsh shadows or high-contrast drama
- Slightly warm white balance for approachability
- Avoid fluorescent or clinical lighting

### Color Treatment
- Aligned with the brand palette: deep ink neutrals, cobalt highlights, and measured teal accents
- Slight desaturation for consistency, with selective high-contrast highlights on key subjects
- Dark, rich full-bleed treatments for trust and seriousness in hero moments
- Light surfaces for product-context imagery to preserve clarity and legibility

## Subject Matter

### People

**Include:**
- Coaches in conversation with clients (1:1 sessions, discovery calls)
- Practice leads reviewing dashboards or team metrics
- Professionals working in calm, organized environments
- Diverse representation (age, ethnicity, gender, ability) across all imagery
- Moments of insight — someone's expression when an idea lands
- Small group coaching scenarios (2-5 people)

**Avoid:**
- Large conference rooms or corporate boardroom setups
- People staring at cameras with forced smiles
- Gym, yoga, or wellness-specific imagery (SpikeForge is not a wellness platform)
- Images of people stressed, overwhelmed, or in chaos (contradicts the brand promise)
- Highly staged "business handshake" clichés

### Environments

**Include:**
- Modern, organized offices with warm textures (wood, plants, natural materials)
- Quiet meeting rooms with good lighting
- Home offices that feel intentional and professional
- Cafés or informal meeting spaces (for coaching conversations)
- Clean desk setups with laptops showing dashboards (not identifiable competitor products)

**Avoid:**
- Open-plan offices with rows of desks
- Clinical, sterile environments
- Cluttered or chaotic spaces
- Outdoors with distracting backgrounds
- Generic "city skyline" or "office building" shots

### Technology

**Include:**
- Clean laptop/tablet screens showing organized dashboards (can be stylized/mocked)
- People referencing devices naturally during conversations
- Phones with notifications (session reminders, client check-ins)
- Technology as a calm background presence, not the hero

**Avoid:**
- Stock "hands typing on keyboard" shots
- Multiple monitors with complex data (feels overwhelming, not simplified)
- Technology as the primary focus — coaching relationships are the hero
- Identifiable third-party software on screens

## Composition Guidelines

### Framing
- Use the rule of thirds — place subjects at intersection points
- Leave breathing room around subjects (generous negative space)
- Shoot at eye level or slightly above for approachable, peer-to-peer framing
- Avoid extreme close-ups or extreme wide shots

### Depth of Field
- Shallow to medium depth of field preferred — subject sharp, background soft
- Keeps focus on people and their interactions
- Creates a clean, professional aesthetic

### Aspect Ratios

| Context | Ratio | Usage |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Hero / Full-width | 16:9 | Landing pages, feature sections |
| Card / Feature | 4:3 | Blog posts, case studies, cards |
| Square | 1:1 | Testimonials, social media, avatars |
| Portrait | 3:4 | Team profiles, coach spotlights |
| Banner | 3:1 | Email headers, narrow hero areas |

## Image Categories

### Hero Images
Full-width, high-impact photography for landing pages and key brand moments.
- Compositions with clear subject hierarchy and deliberate negative space
- Dark-to-light tonal contrast that supports premium product framing
- Single focal point, usually a person, interface, or coached interaction
- Always supports headline overlays with validated contrast-safe zones

### Feature Images
Supporting photography used alongside feature descriptions and platform capabilities.
- Clean, bright treatment
- Medium shot of people in coaching context
- Should complement—not compete with—adjacent UI screenshots

### Testimonial Images
Authentic portraits of coaches and practice leads.
- Environmental portraits (in their workspace)
- Slightly shallow depth of field
- Warm, approachable expression
- Square or 3:4 crop

### Abstract / Texture
Background textures for subtle brand moments.
- Soft multi-stop gradients using cobalt, deep ink, and teal tints
- Clean geometric fields at low opacity for product-like precision
- Minimal organic textures for warmth when narrative context needs it
- Never overwhelming; these remain supporting layers, not focal content

When sourcing stock photography, use coaching-specific search terms:
- "executive coaching session"
- "professional coaching conversation"
- "business mentor meeting"
- "modern office coaching"
- "professional one on one meeting"
- "leadership development"

### Terms to Avoid
- "life coach" (often returns wellness/yoga imagery)
- "therapy session" (clinical connotations)
- "business meeting" (returns corporate clichés)
- "startup office" (too casual, ping-pong tables)

## Image Treatment

### Standard Treatment
- Slight desaturation: -10% to -15% from original
- Warm white balance: +5% warmth
- Subtle vignette for hero images (focus attention)
- No heavy filters, vintage effects, or duotone treatments

### Dark Hero Treatment
For dark-background hero sections:
- Darken highlights, deepen shadows
- Ensure a text-safe dark area (minimum 200px from top/bottom edge)
- Warm light on subjects against cool-dark backgrounds
- Apply subtle teal color overlay at 5-10% opacity for brand cohesion

### Image Overlay
When placing text over images:
- Use a dark gradient overlay (Midnight Ink to transparent)
- Ensure text area meets WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for headlines)
- Prefer bottom-to-top gradient for headlines at the top of images

## Accessibility

- Always provide meaningful `alt` text describing the image content
- Decorative/ambient images use empty `alt=""` with `aria-hidden="true"`
- Ensure text over images meets contrast requirements
- Provide image captions when the image conveys information not in surrounding text
- Avoid images with embedded text — use real text instead

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Feature diverse, authentic-looking professionals
- Use natural light and warm, clean environments
- Show coaching in context — real interactions and real workspaces
- Maintain consistent treatment across all photography
- Use images that support the message, not replace it

### Don't
- Use obviously staged stock photography
- Show people in distress, chaos, or overwhelm
- Use imagery that could be mistaken for a wellness/fitness platform
- Apply heavy filters or trendy effects
- Use images with watermarks or identifiable trademarked content
- Rely on imagery to compensate for unclear messaging
