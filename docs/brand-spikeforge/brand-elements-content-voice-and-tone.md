# SpikeForge Voice & Tone

> How SpikeForge sounds — calm confidence that empowers coaches to run their practice.

## Strategic Foundation

**Brand Alignment:** SpikeForge speaks with the calm confidence of The Guide — the experienced practice operations director who's already figured out how to make everything run smoothly. Our voice is organized, clear, and human. We respect that coaching is deeply personal work and that the operational layer should feel supportive, not corporate. We're direct but never cold, confident but never boastful.

## Voice vs Tone

**Voice** is who we are — consistent across all contexts. It's our personality in words.

**Tone** is how we adapt — it shifts based on context, audience, and situation. A session confirmation sounds different from an error message, but both sound like SpikeForge.

## Voice Characteristics

### 1. Clear
We say exactly what we mean. No jargon, no ambiguity, no filler. Coaches know what to expect.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "3 sessions this week" | "You have multiple upcoming engagements scheduled" |
| "Add a new client" | "Initiate a client onboarding workflow" |
| "This action can't be undone" | "Please note that this operation is irreversible" |

### 2. Confident
We state what we do with conviction, backed by specific capability. We don't hedge or over-qualify.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Session notes saved" | "Your session notes have been successfully saved!" |
| "Your next session is Monday at 2 PM" | "It appears that you may have a session scheduled..." |
| "Invoice sent to Sarah Chen" | "The system has processed and dispatched an invoice..." |

### 3. Empowering
We position coaches as capable professionals who make the decisions. We provide structure, they fill it.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "You already know how to coach. Now your practice can keep up." | "Let us show you the optimal way to manage your practice" |
| "Ready when you are" | "You should complete this step now" |
| "Your practice, your way" | "Follow these best practices for success" |

### 4. Warm
We acknowledge the human side of coaching work. We're friendly without being casual.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Welcome back, Sarah" | "USER_LOGIN_SUCCESS" |
| "Great work — 12 sessions completed this month" | "Monthly session count: 12" |
| "Need a hand? We're here to help" | "Contact support for technical assistance" |

### 5. Practical
We focus on real workflows and real outcomes. We describe what happens, not what the platform is.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Session notes prompt the coach within 24 hours" | "Leverage our AI-powered notification framework" |
| "See which clients haven't had a session in 30 days" | "Access advanced engagement analytics dashboards" |
| "Send an invoice in two clicks" | "Streamline your revenue collection processes" |

## Tone Variations

### Informational
For onboarding, help text, and educational content.

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mood | Helpful, patient, clear |
| Approach | Explain context, provide guidance |
| Example | "Discovery calls have four possible outcomes. The system guides you through each one." |

### Celebratory
For milestones, completions, and positive moments.

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mood | Positive, warm, grounded |
| Approach | Acknowledge the achievement, suggest next steps |
| Example | "Program completed — 12 sessions delivered. Time to review and close out." |

### Cautionary
For warnings, destructive actions, and potential data loss.

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mood | Direct, clear, calm |
| Approach | State the risk, explain the consequence, offer the safe path |
| Example | "Removing this client will delete all session history and notes. This can't be undone." |

### Empathetic
For errors, failures, and frustrating moments.

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mood | Understanding, solution-oriented, honest |
| Approach | Acknowledge the problem, take responsibility, guide to resolution |
| Example | "We couldn't save your session notes. Check your connection and try again — your draft is still here." |

### Persuasive
For upgrades, feature adoption, and conversion moments.

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Mood | Confident, specific, benefit-led |
| Approach | Lead with the coach's outcome, not the feature |
| Example | "See client engagement trends, coach utilization, and revenue at a glance. Upgrade to Practice Insights." |

## Word Choice

### Preferred Terms

| Use | Instead of |
|-----|-----------|
| Client | Customer, user, patient |
| Coach | Provider, practitioner, therapist |
| Practice lead | Admin, administrator, manager |
| Session | Appointment, meeting, event |
| Program | Package, plan, product |
| Practice | Business, company, organization |
| Discovery call | Consultation, intake call |
| Onboard | Set up, register, enroll |
| Action items | Tasks, todos, homework |
| Engagement | Activity, participation |
| Prospect | Lead, inquiry, potential client |

### Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Streamline | Vague, overused SaaS term |
| Leverage | Corporate jargon |
| Optimize | Too technical, too abstract |
| Utilize | Just say "use" |
| Robust | Meaningless without context |
| Seamless | Overused, unverifiable |
| Cutting-edge | Trendy, will date quickly |
| Scalable | Technical, not coach-relevant |

## Voice by Content Type

### Navigation Labels
Short, clear, noun-based. No verbs, no articles.

```
Dashboard | Clients | Sessions | Programs | Billing | Analytics
```

### Button Labels
Action verbs, 1-3 words. Specific about what happens.

```
Schedule Session | Add Client | Save Notes | Send Invoice | Export Report
```

### Empty States
Warm, helpful, action-oriented. Explain what belongs here and how to start.

```
"No sessions this week. Schedule one to keep your client's momentum going."
```

### Error Messages
Empathetic, specific, actionable. Never blame the coach.

```
"We couldn't send the invoice. Check the client's email address and try again."
```

### Confirmation Messages
Brief, specific, confident. State what happened.

```
"Session scheduled — Monday, Jan 15 at 2:00 PM with Sarah Chen."
```

### Onboarding / Help
Patient, clear, step-based. Assume the coach is new to the platform but not to coaching.

```
"Let's set up your practice. First, we'll add your coaching services. Then we'll invite your clients."
```

## Examples by Scenario

### Scheduling a Session
- **Page title:** "Schedule a session"
- **Help text:** "Choose a date, time, and client. You can add session prep notes after scheduling."
- **Confirmation:** "Session scheduled — Thursday, March 6 at 10:00 AM with James Rivera."

### Client at Risk of Disengagement
- **Alert title:** "Client engagement declining"
- **Body:** "James Rivera hasn't had a session in 28 days. Consider reaching out."
- **Action:** "Send check-in" / "View history"

### Program Completion
- **Notification:** "Sarah Chen completed the Leadership Growth program — 12 of 12 sessions."
- **Action:** "Review & close" / "Schedule follow-up"

### Practice Dashboard
- **Greeting:** "Good morning, Sarah. Here's your practice at a glance."
- **Metric labels:** "Active clients" / "Sessions this week" / "Revenue this month"
- **Insight:** "2 clients haven't scheduled their next session."

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Read content aloud — it should sound like a calm, knowledgeable colleague
- Front-load the most important information
- Use "you" and "your" to center the coach
- Be specific — name clients, dates, and outcomes
- Match tone to the emotional context of the moment

### Don't
- Use exclamation points more than once per screen
- Write in third person ("The coach can...")
- Lead with the platform ("SpikeForge allows you to...")
- Use passive voice when active is clearer
- Sacrifice clarity for brevity — if it's confusing, add a word
