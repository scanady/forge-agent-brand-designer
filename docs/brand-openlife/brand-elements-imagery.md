# OpenLife Imagery

> Authentic, purposeful photography that reflects collaboration, innovation, and empowerment.

## Strategic Foundation

**Brand Alignment:** OpenLife's imagery embodies The Liberator archetype—showing people and organizations empowered to innovate. Photography feels confident yet approachable, technical yet human. Every image reinforces that technology serves people, not the other way around.

## Photography Style

### Overall Direction

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| Mood | Confident, forward-looking, collaborative |
| Lighting | Natural, bright, optimistic |
| Color | Clean, true-to-life, slight warmth |
| Composition | Open, balanced, room to breathe |

### Subject Matter

#### People
**Include:**
- Diverse professionals collaborating
- Genuine moments of discussion
- Confident, engaged expressions
- Mixed group sizes
- Cross-generational representation

**Avoid:**
- Stiff corporate poses
- Stereotypical "pointing at screen"
- Homogeneous groups

#### Environments
**Preferred:**
- Modern, open offices
- Technology-enabled spaces
- Co-working environments
- Natural light

**Avoid:**
- Dated aesthetics
- Sterile, cold settings
- Cluttered backgrounds

#### Technology
**Include:**
- Clean, modern interfaces
- Devices as tools, not focus
- Abstract tech representations

**Avoid:**
- Recognizable competitor products
- Binary code clichés
- Technology dominating over people

## Composition Guidelines

### Open Composition
- Generous negative space
- Avoid tight crops
- Leave room for text overlays
- Environmental context

### Rule of Thirds
- Key subjects at intersections
- Leading lines guide the eye
- Avoid static center compositions

### Layering
- Foreground, midground, background
- Subtle depth of field
- Authentic environmental context

## Color Treatment

### Standard Processing
- Slightly lifted shadows
- Natural skin tones
- Clean whites
- Subtle warmth (+5-10%)
- Natural to slightly reduced saturation

### Brand Integration
- Environments may echo brand colors
- Avoid heavy color grading
- Processing should feel invisible

## Stock Photography

### Recommended Sources
| Provider | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Unsplash | High quality, requires curation |
| Pexels | Good diversity, free |
| Getty Images | Premium quality |
| Nappy | Specifically diverse |

### Selection Criteria
- Authentic, not staged
- Modern aesthetics
- Diverse representation
- High resolution (2000px+)
- Commercial license

### Search Terms
- "Modern office collaboration"
- "Diverse tech team"
- "Business innovation"
- "Enterprise technology"

## Image Don'ts

| Category | Avoid |
|----------|-------|
| Clichés | Handshakes, lightbulbs, gears |
| Style | Heavy filters, HDR, vintage |
| Content | Competitor products |
| Quality | Low resolution, pixelated |

## Accessibility

- Provide alt text for all images
- Ensure text over images has contrast
- Don't convey information through images alone
- Test color treatments for visibility

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Select images reflecting OpenLife values
- Maintain style consistency
- Use high-resolution images
- Include diverse representation

### Don't
- Use the same stock image repeatedly
- Apply heavy stylization
- Select conflicting imagery
- Forget licensing verification

## Examples

### Website Hero
- Wide, open composition
- Diverse team in modern setting
- Natural lighting
- Text overlay in quiet zone

### Case Study
- Real customer imagery when possible
- Environmental context
- Candid, professional moments

### Blog
