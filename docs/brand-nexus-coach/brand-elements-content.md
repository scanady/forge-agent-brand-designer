# Nexus Coach Content

> Clear, confident content that respects coaches' time and guides them through their practice.

## Strategic Foundation

**Brand Alignment:** Nexus Coach content embodies The Guide's calm confidence. Every word in the platform should make coaches feel supported, informed, and capable. We write for busy professionals who care about people, not software — our content meets them where they are and respects both their time and their intelligence. Content is the voice of the platform, and it should sound like the experienced operations director described in our brand personality.

## Content Principles

### 1. Clarity Over Cleverness
Coaches should never wonder what a button does, what a field means, or what happens next. Use plain language. Say exactly what you mean. If a coach has to re-read a sentence, we've failed.

### 2. Respect the Coach's Time
Every word should earn its place. Trim unnecessary preambles, filler phrases, and verbose explanations. Coaches have sessions to prepare for and clients to serve — get to the point.

### 3. Empower, Don't Instruct
Position coaches as capable professionals who just need the right information. Say "Your session notes are ready" rather than "You need to review your session notes." The coach decides what to do; we provide the tools.

### 4. Human First, Platform Second
The coaching relationship is the hero, not the software. Frame content around the coach's work, their clients, and their practice — not around features, technology, or platform capabilities.

### 5. Consistent and Predictable
Coaches should learn the platform once, then trust their intuition. Use the same terms, the same patterns, and the same tone across every screen. Consistency builds confidence.

## Content Goals

| Goal | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Guide** | Help coaches understand what to do and what happens next |
| **Inform** | Provide the right information at the right time |
| **Reassure** | Confirm that actions were successful and the practice is running |
| **Empower** | Position coaches as the decision-maker in every interaction |
| **Simplify** | Reduce complexity — make operational tasks feel effortless |

## Content Hierarchy

### Primary Content
The most prominent text on a screen. Should answer: "What am I looking at?"
- Page titles, section headings, primary labels
- Clear, descriptive, action-oriented

### Supporting Content
Context that helps users understand or decide. Should answer: "What should I know?"
- Descriptions, help text, status messages
- Concise, useful, scannable

### System Content
Platform-generated text that reports status or guides flow. Should answer: "What happened?"
- Notifications, confirmations, error messages
- Direct, honest, actionable

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Write in the user's language — "client," "session," "practice," not "entity," "event," "workspace"
- Front-load important information — put the key point first
- Use active voice — "You scheduled a session" not "A session was scheduled"
- Test content with real coaching professionals when possible

### Don't
- Use jargon, technical terms, or marketing speak in the product
- Write long paragraphs in UI context — break into scannable chunks
- Assume coaches know software terminology
- Use different words for the same concept across screens
