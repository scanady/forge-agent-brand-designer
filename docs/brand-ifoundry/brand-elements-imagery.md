# iFoundry Media Imagery

> Photography that reflects the confidence, ambition, and craftsmanship of a technology foundry.

## Strategic Foundation

**Brand Alignment:** iFoundry imagery embodies The Builder archetype — showing the energy, focus, and precision of teams that make real products. Photography should communicate that building great technology is serious, meaningful work done by invested people. The visual tone draws from modern technology brands: bold compositions, high contrast, real working environments, and an emphasis on craft over corporate. Images show the work of building — not generic stock.

## Photography Direction

### Overall Tone
- **Bold, not flashy:** High contrast, strong compositions — confident without shouting
- **Technical, not cold:** Show the human side of engineering — real people solving real problems
- **Modern, not trendy:** Contemporary aesthetics that age well
- **Focused, not cluttered:** Clean compositions with a single clear subject or interaction
- **Real, not staged:** Authentic moments of building, collaborating, and shipping

### Lighting
- High contrast with controlled light — directional lighting with defined shadows
- Warm accent lighting against cool environments for the foundry feel
- Natural light combined with screen glow for workspace shots
- Avoid flat, even lighting — some drama is appropriate for a technology brand

### Color Treatment
- Aligned with the brand palette: cool blues and warm orange accents
- Slightly desaturated backgrounds with saturated subjects for visual focus
- Dark, rich backgrounds for hero and full-bleed contexts
- Light backgrounds for in-app documentation and educational content

## Subject Matter

### People

**Include:**
- Engineers working together at whiteboards, screens, and code
- Architects sketching system diagrams and discussing decisions
- Founders and product leaders in focused conversation with the team
- Diverse representation (age, ethnicity, gender, ability) across all imagery
- Moments of breakthrough — the expression when something works
- Small, intense teams (2–5 people) deeply engaged in the work

**Avoid:**
- Large conference rooms with 20+ people
- People staring at cameras with forced smiles
- Solo stock-photo models pretending to code
- Overly casual environments (beanbags, ping-pong tables, beer fridges)
- Images of stress or chaos — iFoundry brings order, not frenzy

### Environments

**Include:**
- Modern, well-lit workspaces with clean desks and multiple screens
- Whiteboard walls with architecture diagrams and sprint boards
- Dark-mode development environments with code on screen
- Server rooms and infrastructure (when showing scale or reliability)
- Clean, intentional meeting spaces for collaboration

**Avoid:**
- Open-plan offices with rows of identical cubicles
- Corporate boardrooms with mahogany tables
- Cluttered, messy desks (contradicts the Craftsmanship value)
- Generic "city skyline" or "glass building" establishing shots
- Outdoors unless directly relevant (tech events, on-site work)

### Technology

**Include:**
- Clean screens showing code, architecture diagrams, dashboards (can be stylized/mocked)
- Terminal windows with deployment output or build logs
- People referencing devices naturally during working sessions
- Hardware context when relevant — servers, infrastructure, development machines
- Technology as a tool of craft, not a decoration

**Avoid:**
- Stock "hands typing on keyboard" close-ups
- Screens showing identifiable third-party software
- Binary code / matrix-style backgrounds (cliché)
- AI-generated robots or humanoid tech imagery
- Technology as the sole focus — the builder + the technology together is the hero

## Composition Guidelines

### Framing
- Use the rule of thirds — place subjects at intersection points
- Slightly tight compositions that create energy and forward momentum
- Shoot at eye level or slightly low for a sense of stature and capability
- Allow negative space for text overlay on hero images

### Depth of Field
- Shallow to medium depth of field — subject sharp, background contextual
- Bokeh of screens and lights creates an ambient tech environment
- Use depth to create layers of activity — focus on the primary subject

### Aspect Ratios

| Context | Ratio | Usage |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Hero / Full-width | 16:9 | Landing pages, feature sections |
| Card / Feature | 4:3 | Case studies, blog posts, cards |
| Square | 1:1 | Social media, team profiles |
| Portrait | 3:4 | Team profiles, engineer spotlights |
| Banner | 3:1 | Email headers, narrow hero areas |

## Image Categories

### Hero Images
Full-width, high-impact photography for landing pages and key brand moments.
- Dark-toned backgrounds with dramatic subject lighting
- Single focal point — people building, shipping, or collaborating
- Should support text overlay (ensure contrast-safe dark areas)
- Blue and orange accent lighting where possible for brand cohesion

### Case Study/ Feature Images
Photography used alongside project descriptions and technical capabilities.
- Clean, bright or medium-contrast treatment
- Show teams in the context of the work described
- Include subtle technology context (screens, whiteboards, diagrams)

### Team / People Images
Authentic portraits of the iFoundry team and engineers.
- Environmental portraits — in their workspace, at their setup
- Medium depth of field with workspace context
- Confident, focused expression — engaged in the craft
- Square or 3:4 crop

### Abstract / Texture
Background textures for subtle brand moments.
- Dark gradients incorporating Forge Blue to Iron Slate
- Angular geometric patterns at low opacity (echoing the logo's connected nodes)
- Subtle grid or dot patterns suggesting precision and structure
- Never overwhelming — these are atmosphere, not content

## Image Treatment

### Standard Treatment
- Slight contrast boost: +10% for modern punch
- Cool white balance with warm accents on subjects
- Subtle desaturation on backgrounds: -10% to -15%
- No heavy filters, vintage effects, or duotone treatments

### Dark Hero Treatment
For dark-background hero sections:
- Deep shadows, controlled highlights on subjects
- Text-safe dark area (minimum 200px from edges)
- Blue-tinted shadows for cohesion with the palette
- Apply subtle blue-to-transparent gradient overlay at 5–10% opacity

### Image Overlay
When placing text over images:
- Use a dark gradient overlay (Iron Slate to transparent)
- Ensure text area meets WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for headlines)
- Prefer bottom-to-top gradient for headlines at the top of images

## Accessibility

- Always provide meaningful `alt` text describing the image content
- Decorative/ambient images use empty `alt=""` with `aria-hidden="true"`
- Ensure text over images meets contrast requirements
- Provide image captions when the image conveys information not in surrounding text
- Avoid images with embedded text — use real text instead

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Feature diverse, authentic-looking technology professionals
- Use high-contrast lighting that creates energy and focus
- Show people building — real interactions over posed settings
- Maintain consistent treatment across all photography
- Use dark-toned hero images for bold, foundry-inspired brand moments

### Don't
- Use obviously staged stock photography of people pretending to work
- Show people in stress, confusion, or overwhelm
- Use cliché "tech" imagery (binary code, robot hands, blue circuit boards)
- Apply heavy filters or trendy effects
- Use images with watermarks or identifiable trademarked content
- Rely on imagery to compensate for unclear messaging
