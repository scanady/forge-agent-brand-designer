# iFoundry Media Logos

> A mark that connects disparate elements into something unified — the visual signature of a technology foundry.

## Strategic Foundation

**Brand Alignment:** The iFoundry logo embodies The Builder — bold, structural, and engineered with purpose. The logomark features circular nodes connected by angular lines to a bold lowercase "i," representing how iFoundry draws disparate elements — ideas, technologies, people — together and forges them into a unified product. The visual language of connected nodes conveys both technical precision and the partnership model at the core of the brand.

## Logo Concept

### The Forge Mark
The logomark is built from circular nodes connected by angular lines converging on a bold lowercase "i." This represents the foundry process: raw, separate elements being drawn together, connected, and forged into something greater. The blue nodes (intelligence, architecture) and orange/red energy lines (momentum, transformation) create a dual-color mark that captures both the technical depth and the creative heat of the foundry.

### Design Principles
- **Angular precision with organic connection:** Sharp lines meeting circular nodes — engineering meets partnership
- **Dual-color identity:** Forge Blue and Ignite Orange together — always
- **Scalable clarity:** Legible from favicon (16px) to large-format signage
- **Bold simplicity:** The "i" is unmistakable; the connected nodes are distinct at any size
- **Forward momentum:** The composition suggests convergence and formation

## Incorrect Usage

The following treatments compromise the logo's integrity and must be avoided.

1. **Don't stretch or distort** — Maintain original proportions at all times
2. **Don't rotate** — The logo should always appear level
3. **Don't change the colors** — Use only approved color variations
4. **Don't add effects** — No drop shadows, gradients, bevels, or glows
5. **Don't outline** — The logo is not a stroke treatment
6. **Don't place on busy backgrounds** — Ensure sufficient contrast and legibility
7. **Don't rearrange elements** — The mark and wordmark have fixed spatial relationships
8. **Don't crop the mark** — The logomark must always appear complete with all nodes
9. **Don't change the typeface** — The wordmark uses Space Grotesk Bold exclusively
10. **Don't capitalize the "i"** — It's always lowercase; this is a brand rule, not a typo
11. **Don't separate the mark's colors** — The blue/orange combination is integral to the identity
12. **Don't animate without approval** — Motion treatments require brand team review
13. **Don't place below minimum size** — Small logos become illegible

## Tagline Usage

**"Forged to perform."** is the primary tagline and may appear alongside the logo, but never inside or attached to the logo.

### Tagline Placement
- Below the primary logo with 1.5x clear space separation
- Tagline font: Space Grotesk Medium, 40–50% of wordmark height
- Color: Slate 500 on light backgrounds, Slate 300 on dark backgrounds

### Tagline Format
- Sentence case: "Forged to perform."
- Always include the period
- Never modify the tagline text

## Co-Branding

When the iFoundry logo appears alongside partner or client logos:
- Maintain equal visual weight between logos
- Separate logos with a vertical divider (1px, Slate 200) or generous spacing (minimum 2x clear space)
- Never modify the iFoundry logo to match partner styling
- The iFoundry logo should appear first (left or top) when we are the primary brand
- In partnership contexts, both logos should have equal prominence

## Usage Guidelines

### Do
- Use the SVG version whenever possible for crisp rendering
- Maintain minimum clear space in all contexts
- Choose the color variation that provides highest contrast against the background
- Use the Stacked variation for square or compact contexts
- Use the Logomark Only for established-brand contexts (app icons, favicons, watermarks)
- Always maintain the lowercase "i" in the wordmark

### Don't
- Recreate the logo from scratch — always use approved files
- Use low-resolution raster files when vector is available
- Place the logo where it could be confused with navigation
- Use the full Primary logo in spaces smaller than 140px wide
- Combine the logo with other graphic elements or icons
- Use "IFoundry" — the lowercase "i" is non-negotiable
